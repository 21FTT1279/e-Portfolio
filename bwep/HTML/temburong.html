Temburong




       




Belalong Canopy Walkway

Cross five interconnected towers with a canopy height of more than 43 meters.
From up here, you can get a bird's eye perspective of Brunei's rainforests, which are home to one of the world's most diversified ecosystems.



Sultan Haji Omar Ali Saifuddien Bridge

Sultan Haji Omar 'Ali Saifuddien Bridge, which stretches over 30 kilometers, is one of Southeast Asia's longest bridges.
The Temburong neighborhood was connected to the mainland via a dual-carriageway bridge that opened in March 2020.



Sumbiling Eco Village

Sumbiling Eco Village is a jungle camp with modest utilities located along a river.
It has wonderful Iban food, interesting outdoor activities such as inner tubing, and night/dawn treks that allow you to connect with nature.



Sungai Apan Waterfall

The first tier of the Sungai Apan Waterfall is accessible by a 40-minute longboat journey followed by a 20-minute climb.
The trek will be lengthy and exhausting, but the sight of the waterfall will provide much-needed relief.



Bangar Town

Bangar Town is a pleasant pause on the ever-moving tale of our life.
A thrilling 45-minute speedboat journey away, Bangar is hidden between thick mangrove forest and its lush greenery of nipa palms.
