Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque

Brunei's most iconic picture is the Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque in Bandar Seri Begawan.
The mosque, which overlooks the Brunei River and Kampong Ayer, is surrounded by an artificial lagoon/reflecting pool.
This is one of Brunei's most photogenic sights.



Kampong Ayer

Kampong Ayer is consistently ranked as one of Brunei's finest tourist destinations, offering an experience unlike any other.
A maze-like network of wooden boardwalks connects more than 40 minor communities in the medieval floating village.
Kampong Ayer is home to 13,000 people who have their own schools, mosques, and stores.



Royal Regalia Museum

The museum's architectural design incorporates the Malay Islamic Monarchy idea while also displaying Brunei Darussalam's royal regalia and customs.
There are four primary galleries in the museum.

    Royal Coronation
    Royal Exhibition
    Silver Jubilee
    Golden Jubilee



Malay Technology Museum

Another must-see in Brunei for anyone interested in learning about the country's culture.
A collection of traditional garments, as well as exhibitions on Malay and Bruneian ethnography, may be seen in many rooms.
Others depict how residents of the floating hamlet live. This museum will take around an hour to explore.



Twelve roofs/Bubungan dua belas

One of Brunei's oldest structures is the former High Commissioner's home.
The colonial organization, which dates back to 1906, has strong connections to imperial Britain.
The Twelve Roofs House became a museum after the country gained independence in 1984.
Inside, you'll discover about the amicable connection between the United Kingdom and Brunei, as well as the bond between the Queen and the Sultan.



Jerudong Park

Take a rollercoaster ride with friends and have a picnic in the garden, or take a lovely boat trip with someone special and spend some time together.
At the water park, you may cool down and refresh your feet, or enjoy a fanciful stroll around the park's gorgeous sceneries.
Before you go, don't forget to snap a photo at the Diamond Monument!



Muara Beach

The greatest beach in Brunei is around 30 minutes away by vehicle from Bandar Seri Begawan.
There are various picnic seats, a jogging route, and a good spot to observe Borneo's spectacular sunset along the pristine length of shoreline and nearby parks.
